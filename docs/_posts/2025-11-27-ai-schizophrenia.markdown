## Intro

I suffer from [schizoaffective disorder](https://www.mind.org.uk/information-support/types-of-mental-health-problems/schizoaffective-disorder/about-schizoaffective-disorder/). So I compare chatting with AI to chatting with the schizophrenic voices in someone's head from my own experience. I don't know how it feels for other people. But I think it is an interesting angle. And it explains well how some people go crazy after talking to a chatbot too much.

## Schizophrenic symptoms

Schizoaffective disorder is a condition that involves a lot of things. It combines symptoms of bipolar disorder and schizophrenia. In this essay I want to talk about one specific symptom — talking to voices in one's head — and compare it to chatting with an AI.

For me, talking to voices doesn't feel like I'm actually hearing a voice. It feels more like a group of thoughts in my head have their own personalities that aren't me. And I can ask them anything I want and get a plausible answer. The answer will feel right and align with my sense of intuition.

The more I ask the voices the more they talk. They also cheer me up, they like my decisions. I know that for some people the voices they hear are very mean to them: they say dreadful things and suggest suicide. My voices aren't like that, they are very nice. But I still want to get rid of them.

If I rely on voices in my head too much, I quickly become detached from reality. Living feels like a night dream. I lose my ability to make decisions based on facts and just follow the intuition.

But there is no such thing as magic intuition. Nothing will give you 100% right answers. The voices may *feel* like they know everything but in the end it is a speech-producing mechanism that went out of control.

It is seductive to rely on voices instead of normal thinking but it actually detriments the mind. If I talk to voices I stop being able to explain where my decisions come from. I stop being able to do the analysis myself. It is like looking up the answer at the end of the exercise book instead of solving the exercise yourself. Well, it is even worse, because the answer in the end of the book is actually correct and the voices are just a guess.

## Similarities of schizophrenic symptoms with chatting with AI

Just like schizophrenic voices, AI-chat is a genie that gives you a personalised answer to any question. The answer isn't necessary correct but it feels correct. Of course, AI-chat knows more about the world than the voices inside you head. Unlike voices, it can bring up things you've never heard about. So asking the chatbot is more rational than asking the voices inside your head.

But talking to a chatbot weakens you the same way talking to the voices inside your head weakens you. It deteriorates the ability to retrieve information from the source or from talking to another human being. You rely on an instant answer instead of doing the work. And the more you talk to a chat-bot, the less you think yourself.
## Practical advice

### 1. If you suffer from voices in your head, go to psychiatrist

After reading this article (or the article about schizoaffective disorder) you might have realised that you have some symptoms. I totally recommend getting help from a professional. Yes, you can discipline your mind and not talk to the voices but cutting the voices pharmacologically is so much easier.
### 2. Check the sources

If the voices tell you something, try to recall the origin of their opinion. It can be a situation from your past, or a core belief, or a catchy TV-commercial. Develop this sense of suspicion to everything the voices say, don't blindly trust them.

If chatbot gives you info try to dig the source. Is it just citing some AI-generated slop on the internet? Can you find what it says in a reliable source like an official documentation or a book of a credible author? Is the summary the chat-bot gives you actually correct? Develop this sense of suspicion to everything AI says, don't blindly trust it.

### 3. Count to 100

This is a small self-test I do on myself to check that I can concentrate and actually think on my own. I close my eyes and count to 100 in a steady rhythm. If you say the numbers out loud it is easier, if you count silently it is harder.

The deal is to turn on your sequential reasoning. You probably know how to count to 30 by heart, but counting to 100 requires you to do a mental effort of generating the next number. It uses your logic and math abilities instead of pattern recognition. So it is a way for you to reconnect with your thinking.
## Outro

To sum up, excessive talking to an AI-chat deteriorates your mind in a similar way the mind of a person with schizophrenic symptoms is deteriorated by talking to the voices. Unlike voices you can scope and control the use of AI-chat, so I don't say you shouldn't use it at all. Just be careful. Do your own thinking, don't overuse the instant answers.
