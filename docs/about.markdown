# About page

On this page you'll find more info about me. I try to keep it updated.

## age, sex, pronouns

I'm 30 y.o. woman. I was assigned girl at birth and I totally agree with it. My pronouns are she/her.

## origin, residence, spoken languages

I'm an expat in Amsterdam. I live in Amsterdam for more than 5 years. I love the Netherlands very much and I plan to have it as my primary residence for the rest of my life. I might move to another city but it will be within the Netherlands.

I originally come from Russia. I was born in Moscow. I got my Bachelor's in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics from Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology. I learned a lot from my time in Russia but I don't feel like I want to grow old in Moscow or anywhere else in Russia. 

I think that my primary identity is the expat identity. Being born in Russia is just a flavour of it. I consider myself to be a global citizen.

I'm fluent in Russian and English. During the course of my life I got certified B2 in Dutch and B1 in Spanish but I don't consider these languages to be the languages I can use for work.

## personal life

I'm happily married to a man I met during my studies at university. However, we have different opinions on having kids. I want kids and my husband doesn't. So I'm passively looking for a person to raise kids together. When I find one, I divorce my husband to join the new family.

I'm bisexual. I'm also open to poly-relationships. I want to raise from 2 to 5 kids and I want to give birth to at least one of them unless there are severe medical contraindications.

## mental health

I'm diagnosed with [schizoaffective disorder](https://www.mind.org.uk/information-support/types-of-mental-health-problems/schizoaffective-disorder/about-schizoaffective-disorder/). In practice it means that if I don't take my pills, I hear voices chatting in my head and telling me what to do. I also suffer from the side-effects of my pills, like increased sleepiness.

Why do I even mention this diagnosis? Because it's an important part of how I see the world. Sometimes I bring it up in my essays: there are things I want to be said that aren't possible to express without mentioning this experience. Overall, thanks to this sickness I'm extremely aware of my thinking patterns. I believe that sharing the insights I got  is a better choice than carefully hiding my condition out of fear of stigma.

## personality

I'm smart, stubborn and organised.

I'm creative and proactive. I don't hesitate to take the initiative or reaching out to people.

I hate injustice and I fix it where I can. For example, I call out cruel jokes.

I value quality over quantity. I value working with my own hands. I think local, not global. I want to make the world better by improving one thing at a time.

I'm honest, loyal and transparent.

I keep myself to a high (but achievable) standard. I love to exceed expectations.

But on top of all that I'm cute, playful and cheerful. Really! Most of the time I don't think about my dead serious core values. I just explore, play and have fun!
